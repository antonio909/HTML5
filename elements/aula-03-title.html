The title element The title element represent's the document's title or name. Authors should use title that identify their documents even when they are used out of context, for example in a user's history of bookmarks, or in search results. The document's title is often different from its first heading, since the first heading does not have to stand alone when taken out of context. There must be no more than one title element per document. Example: Here are some examples of appropriate title, contrasted with the top-level headings that might be used on those same pages.
